Global Table:
Indian Cuisine
Without Borders
Hyderabad

In the bustling kitchens of the city and the quiet courtyards of old Hyderabad, cuisine reflects centuries of trade, migration, and cultural fusion. A single plate here might blend Mughal refinement, Deccani soul, and street-food boldness—all wrapped in the aroma of saffron and preserved spices.

Local cooks still use traditional clay pots and charcoal fires. Recipes are passed down by gesture. Food, more than anything, is about generosity—meals are shared with chai and stories that stretch into the evening.

Seekh Kebab

The art of the kebab lies in its simplicity and precision. Ground spiced meat, shaped by hand on a skewer, kissed by charcoal flame. Every bite tells the story of generations.

Meat
Mutton
Grill Time
12 min
Heat
900°C
Charcoal + Spice + Time = Memory

Our kebabs are hand-rolled each service, no mass production. We rest the meat mixture for 4 hours minimum, letting the spices bloom into the fibre of the meat. The grill is set to exactly 900 degrees—measured, not guessed.

RECIPE STUDIO

Cook
Along

Take this home. We've written our dal recipe for the ambitious cook—not simplified, not diluted. This is what our kitchen makes, step by step, ingredient by ingredient.

Base
Black lentils (urad dal) · 250g
Red kidney beans (rajma) · 100g
Ginger-garlic paste · 2 tbsp
Green chilli · 3 nos
Spice
Cumin seeds · 1 tsp
Cinnamon stick · 1 inch
Cardamom · 4 pods
Bay leaf · 2

18-Hour
Dal Makhani

Step 1: Soak lentils and beans for 30 minutes. Boil separately until 60% cooked (lentils: 1.5 hours, beans: 2 hours).

Step 2: Toast cumin, cinnamon, cardamom, bay leaf in ghee for 45 seconds. Add ginger-garlic paste, cook 3 minutes on low heat.

Step 3: Add cooked dal and beans to the tadka. Add salt, red chilli powder (1 tsp), and turmeric (1/2 tsp). Mix well.

Step 4: Slow simmer for 16 hours on the lowest heat (or in a clay pot buried in embers, traditionally). Stir every 2 hours.

Step 5: Add butter (100g) and fresh cream (150ml) in the last hour. Fold in gently. Garnish with fresh coriander and kasuri methi.

The magic happens in the waiting. This dal cannot be rushed.

BEHIND THE KITCHEN

Process,
Craft,
Devotion

Every dish at TreeOak is built on three pillars: obsessive process, refined craft, and genuine devotion to ingredient and tradition.

Process
Measured. Repeatable. No shortcuts. Every spice is weighed, every ingredient sourced from trusted suppliers, every step timed to the minute.
Craft
Years of training. Our head chef learned under masters in Lucknow and Hyderabad. Technique is passed hand to hand, not downloaded.
Devotion
We are not just feeding hunger. We are honouring tradition, feeding memory, creating moments that linger long after the plate is cleared.
